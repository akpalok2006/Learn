PHRASE (Nothing But)
You use nothing but in front of a noun, an infinitive without ‘to’, or an ‘-ing’ form to mean ‘only’.

Use of Nothing But

Rule: Sub+do/does+nothing+But+infinitive

  1. I do nothing but play: मैं खेलता ही रहता हूँ
  2. He does nothing but sleep: वह सोता ही रहता है
  3. Sanjeev does nothing but think: संजीव सोचता ही रहता है
  4. The children do nothing but laugh: बच्चे हस्ते ही रहते है
  5. You do nothing but work: तुम काम करते ही रहते हो
  6. Do I do nothing but play: क्या मैं खेलता ही रहता हूँ

7.  Does he do nothing but sleep? क्या वह सोने के अलावा कुछ नहीं करता?

8.  Why do you do nothing but work? आप काम के अलावा कुछ क्यों नहीं करते?

9.  Do you do nothing but learn English: क्या आप अंग्रेजी सीखने के अलावा कुछ नहीं करते

If I were you, I would + (verb)

Here you are giving an example of what decision YOU would do in given the circumstances. This can be in past tense or in a conditional present.

  • If I were you, I would enjoy my vacation.
  • If I were you, I would explain what happened.
  • Yes, If I were you, I would continue working until it is done.
  • If I were you, I would book my reservations now.
  • If I were you, I would answer the question.

By adding ‘have’ after the word ‘would’ you are talking about something in the past tense.

  • If I were you, I would have enjoyed my vacation.
  • If I were you, I would have explained what happened.
  • Yes, If I were you, I would have continued working until it was done.
  • If I were you, I would have booked my reservations now.
  • If I were you, I would have answered the question.
