Use of Would have

These past tense modals are useful for expressing your present feelings about a past decision (or other action). (Lesson-23)

To form these past modals would be followed by have, followed by a past participle verb (Sub+would have+v3+obj). Use have for all pronouns; never use has or had to form a past modal. Here are some examples:

I would have gone to the party, but I was tired. मैं पार्टी में गया होता, लेकिन मैं थक गया था।

I would have called, but there was no phone service. मैं फ़ोन करता /किया होता लेकिन कोई फ़ोन सर्विस नहीं था

If she would have been faithful अगर वह वफादार होती

I would have loaned you the money, but I didn’t have any. मैंने आपको पैसे उधर दे दिया होता लेकिन मेरे पास पैसा नहीं था

I would have attended your party, but I was in a different city. मैंने आपकी पार्टी में भाग लिया होता, लेकिन मैं अलग शहर में था।

If you would have told me. अगर आपने मुझे बताया होता

I would have gone to the party, but I was not feeling well. मैं पार्टी में गया होता, लेकिन मेरी तबियत ठीक नहीं थी

I would have called you, but I did not have your number. मैंने आपको फोन किया होता/करता, लेकिन मेरे पास आपका नंबर नहीं था. (Lesson-23)

Vocabulary

Mess  /mes/  noun (DIRT/UNTIDINESS): Something or someone that is a mess, or is in a mess, looks dirty or untidy.

1 He makes a terrible mess when he’s cooking. 2 Jem’s house is always in a mess. 3 Go and clean up that mess in the kitchen.

4 My hair’s such a mess today!

Tangled /ˈtæŋ.ɡəld/ adjective  (twisted into an untidy mass) उलझा हुआ

1 Her tangled hair.  2 Your bedclothes are all tangled up. 3 She ran a comb through her tangled hair.

Gratitude /ˈɡræt̬.ə.tuːd/ noun (the feeling or quality of being grateful) आभार, कृतज्ञता, धन्यवाद

1 I extend gratitude to my parrents for their love and support. 2 Take this as a token of my gratitude for all your help.

Comfy  /ˈkʌm.fi/ adjective  (informal for comfortable) आरामदायक   a comfy chair

Monk noun /mʌŋk/   a member of a group of religious men who do not marry and usually live together in a monastery साधु, मठवासी, संन्यासी

Monastery noun /ˈmɑː.nə.ster.i/ (a building in which monks live and worship) मठ, विहार

Fury noun  /ˈfjʊr.i/ (extreme anger) 1 She flew into a fury at the suggestion. 2 He was blind with fury. 3 The fury of the crowd was directed towards the police.

Furious adjective /ˈfʊr.i.əs/  (extremely angry)  गुस्से से आग बबूला होना, बहुत गुस्से में होना 1I was late and he was furious with me.

Slumber noun /ˈslʌm.bɚ/  (sleep) नींद, निद्रा, 1 I fell into a gentle slumber. 2 I didn’t want to rouse you from your slumbers. (Lesson-23)

I’m dying to + (verb)

When using the word ‘dying’ in this manner, you are referring to wanting or desiring something greatly.

  • I’m dying to relax on the beach.
  • I’m dying to pick some fresh fruit.
  • He is dying to order some desserts.
  • I’m dying to find out if I got the job.
  • I’m dying to move to a bigger house.
  • They are dying to look at all the work you’ve done.
  • I’m dying to learn more about you.
  • I’m dying to introduce you to my parents.
  • She is dying to expand her business.
  • I’m dying to check my score on the test.
