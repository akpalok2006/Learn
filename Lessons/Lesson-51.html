Sentences lesson-51

  • The Teacher got the math solved by students.
  • The Manager didn’t get the report prepared by me.
  • He does not get his clothes washed by others.
  • Why don’t you get your work completed by the team?
  • How do you get all the work done by the team?
  • When do you get all presentations prepared by him?

Vocabulary

Mend verb (/mend/) सुधार to repair something that is broken or damaged: Could you mend this hole in my shirt? The plumber came to mend the burst pipe. The country’s president is seeking to mend relations with the United States. (lesson-51)

Feasible adjective /ˈfiː.zə.bəl/ संभव Able to be made, done, or achieved: 1-With the extra resources, the plan now seems feasible. 2-It’s not feasible to dismiss him.

 Adapt /əˈdæpt/ अनुकूल बनना/बनाना To change, or to change something, to suit different conditions or uses: 1- Many software companies have adapted popular programs to the new operating system. 2-She knew how to adapt herself. 3- You should adapt yourself to the new class.

Slake verb /sleɪk/ प्यास शांत करना/इच्छा शांत करना To satisfy a feeling of being thirsty or of wanting something: 1-I don’t think Dick will ever manage to slake his lust for power. 2-Nothing can slake his anger.

Impart verb /ɪmˈpɑːrt/ बताना/देना to communicate information to someone: 1- to impart the bad news. 2-I was rather quiet as I didn’t feel I had much wisdom to impart on the subject. 2-A teacher’s aim is to impart knowledge. 3- I have no news to impart. 4- Condiments impart flavor to food. (भोजन के लिए मसाला स्वाद प्रदान करता है।) (lesson-51)

Vocabulary

Voracious adjective /vəˈreɪ.ʃəs/ पेटू, भुक्खड़ Very eager for something, especially a lot of food: 1- He has a voracious appetite (= he eats a lot). 2- He’s a voracious reader of historical novels (= he reads a lot of them eagerly and quickly). Needing a lot of something to be satisfied: 1- As a child, I had a voracious appetite for books.

Appetite noun /ˈæp.ə.taɪt/ भूख, इच्छा, रूचि The feeling that you want to eat food: 1- All that walking has given me an appetite. 2- I won’t have any chocolate, thanks. It will spoil (= reduce) my appetite. 3- I took a speed-reading class and developed a voracious appetite for books.

Dwindle verb /ˈdwɪn.dəl/ घटना, कम होना, क्षीण होना To become smaller in size or amount, or fewer in number: 1- If you don’t use your passion, it’s going to dwindle.

Hitch noun /hɪtʃ/ अड़चन, रुकावट A temporary difficulty that causes a short delay: 1- They achieved their goal without a hitch.
