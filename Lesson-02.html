Would you mind? and Do you mind? lesson-02

We use the phrases would you mind + -ing form, and do you mind + -ing form to ask people politely to do things. Would you mind is more polite and more common:

1-Would you mind opening the window, please? 2-Do you mind turning down the volume a little, please? 3-Do you mind me turning on the light? (I want to turn on the light)

Mind as a verb

We use the verb mind to mean ‘take care or be careful of or about something, or ‘pay attention to something’. In this meaning, we usually use it in the imperative: 1-Mind your step! 2-Mind your head. This is a very small door! 3-Kathy, mind that you don’t trip over my bag. It’s right by the door. (We don’t use the word mind to mean remember: We must mind that)
