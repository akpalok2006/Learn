Vocabulary

Crucial adjective /ˈkruː.ʃəl/ extremely important or necessary: 1-a crucial decision/question. 2-Her work has been crucial to the project’s success. 3-Price will be a crucial factor in the success of this new product.

Compel verb /kəmˈpel/ to force someone to do something. 1-As a schoolboy teachers are compelling us to wear shorts even in winter. 2-The new circumstances compelled a change in policy. (lesson-52)

Compelling adjective /kəmˈpel.ɪŋ/ very exciting and interesting and make you want to watch or listen: 1-I found the whole film very compelling

Lick verb /lɪk/ (MOVE TONGUE) to move the tongue across the surface of something: 1-He licked the chocolate off his fingers. 2-She licked the stamps and stuck them on the parcel.

Fanatic noun /fəˈnæt̬.ɪk/ दीवाना,कट्टरपंथी A person who is extremely interested in something, to a degree that some people find unreasonable: 1- I am not a religious fanatic, but I am a Christian (मैं धार्मिक कट्टर नहीं हूं, लेकिन मैं ईसाई हूं।) 2-He’s a real football fanatic.

Commotion noun /kəˈmoʊ.ʃən/ हल्ला गुल्ला, शोर-ग़ुल, तहलका, खलबली, विद्रोह A sudden, short period of noise, confusion, or exciting movement: 1- His arrival caused quite a commotion (उनके आने से काफी हंगामा हुआ). 2-The people upstairs were making a great commotion (ऊपर बैठे लोगों ने जमकर हंगामा किया।). 3- She had created a commotion at the registration area (उसने रजिस्ट्रेशन एरिया में हंगामा किया था।).
