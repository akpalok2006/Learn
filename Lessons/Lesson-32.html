Vocabulary

Bear verb (/ber/) (bore | borne or US also born) (सहना। धारण करना। जन्म देना। बर्दास्त करना। फल देना। पैदा करना।)To accept, tolerate, or endure something, especially something unpleasant: 1- Tell me now! I can’t bear the suspense! 2-It’s your decision – you have to bear the responsibility if things go wrong. 3-He couldn’t bear to see the dog in pain. 4-I will bear the responsibility for whatever happens.

To have or continue to have something:

1-Thank you for your advice – I’ll bear it in mind (= I will remember and consider it). To hold or support something: The chair, too fragile to bear her weight, collapsed. To give birth to young, or (of a tree or plant) to give or produce fruit or flowers: 1-She had borne six children by the time she was 30. 2-When his wife bore him a child, he could not hide his delight.जब उसकी पत्नी ने उसे एक बच्चे को जन्म दिया, तो वह अपनी खुशी को छिपा नहीं सका।) 3-The pear tree they planted has never borne fruit. To carry and move something to a place: At Christmas, the family all arrive at the house bearing gifts.

Elated adjective /iˈleɪ.t̬ɪd/ Extremely happy and excited, often because something has happened or been achieved: 1-She was elated at/by the news. 2-They were elated at the result. 3-Her success elated the family.

Eject verb /iˈdʒekt/ To force someone to leave a particular place: 1-Officials used guard dogs to eject the protesters. 2-Press the stop button again to eject the tape. 3-The pilot managed to eject moments before the plane crashed.

Gravitate verb /ˈɡræv·ɪˌteɪt/ To be attracted to or move toward something: 1-Artists gravitate toward one another. 2-Many young people gravitate to the cities in search of work. 3-Boys gravitate towards girls at that age.

Pounce verb /paʊns/ दबोचना, झपटना, आक्रमण करना To jump or move quickly in order to catch or take hold of something: 1-The cat sat in the tree ready to pounce on the ducks below. 2-The police were waiting to pounce when he arrived at the airport.

Profusely adv /prəˈfjuːs.li/ बहुत, अधिक मात्रा में In large amounts: 1-She apologized/thanked us profusely. 2-He was bleeding/sweating profusely.

Before she had time to realize what was happening, they pounced upon her. Pulled her into the bushes and began to beat her with a lead pipe. One boy continually kicked her in the face until she was bleeding profusely.

Past Perfect Tense Lesson-32

The past perfect is a part of the “perfect” tense, it expresses completed actions. That is, the action that started in the past is finished,

पहचान: चूका था, चुकी थी, चुके थे |(Lesson-32)

यदि  भूतकाल  में  दो  कार्य  हुआ  हो  ;   एक  कार्य  दुसरे  कार्य  के  बहुत  पहले  पूरी  तरह  समाप्त  हो गया  हो  ,   तो  पहले  समाप्त  होने  वाले कार्य  के  लिए   Past  Perfect  Tense  की  क्रिया  का  प्रयोग  होता  हैं  और  बाद  में  समाप्त  होनेवाले  कार्य  के  लिए   Past Indefinite  Tense  की  क्रिया  का  प्रयोग  होता  हैं .

When Two actions are there and both are interdependent. The first action has completely happened, Past perfect tense is used for the first completed action. For the last action Past Indefinite Tense is used

Like: The patient had died before the doctor came
