Vocabulary

Knack noun /næk/ Skill or an ability to do something easily and well: She has the knack of making people feel comfortable. 2- John had an incredible knack for nicely summing it all up, didn’t he? (lesson-34)

Futile adjective /ˈfjuː.t̬əl/ व्यर्थ, निष्फल, निरर्थक (of actions) having no effect or achieving nothing: -1 All my attempts to cheer her up proved futile. 2-Without it, all efforts are futile

Bolster verb /ˈboʊl.stɚ/ To support or improve something or make it stronger: 1- You need to bolster your conviction. 2-More money is needed to bolster the industry उद्योग को गति देने के लिए अधिक धन की आवश्यकता है). 3-The pillar bolsters the roof.

Incongruent adjective /ɪnˈkɑːŋ.ɡru.ənt/ Not suitable or not fitting well with something else: 1- Chewing tobacco was massive incongruent with the quality of a man he had become. 2- Makeshift English communication skill is massively incongruent with your professional standard.

Bizarre adjective /bɪˈzɑr/ अजीब, विचित्र, अनोखा Very strange and unusual: 1- A bizarre situation/behavior. 2- I found the whole situation very bizarre.

Ecstasy noun /ˈek.stə.si/ परमानंद, अति आनन्द A state of extreme happiness, especially when feeling pleasure: 1- My father was in ecstasy when I won a scholarship. 2- He closed his eyes in ecstasy.

Savor adjective /ˈseɪ.vɚ/ स्वाद लेना, मज़ा लेना To enjoy food or an experience slowly, in order to enjoy it as much as possible: 1-The soup has a savor of onion. 2- You need to savor this wine to get its full flavor.

Muster verb /ˈmʌs.tɚ/ इकट्ठा करना, जुटाना To produce or encourage something such as an emotion or support: 1-She managed to muster the courage to ask him to the cinema. 2- He mustered every ounce of energy he had left. 3- We muster the courage to attain our goal.

Grave adjective /ɡreɪv/ गंभीर, भयानक, चिंताजनक Seriously bad: 1-A grave situation (गंभीर स्थिति). 2-The problem does not seem so grave (समस्या इतनी गंभीर नहीं लगती). 3-This could have grave consequences (इसके गंभीर परिणाम हो सकते हैं). 4-He expressed grave concern (उन्होंने गंभीर चिंता व्यक्त की).

Resent verb /rɪˈzent/ बुरा मानना, नाराज होना, क्रोध करना To feel angry because you have been forced to accept someone or something that you do not like: 1- I resented his interference in my work. 2- I really resent the way he treated me.

Present Perfect Continuous Tense

पहचान: रहा है, रही है,रहे हैं,+ समय

Present Perfect Continuous tense represents the work that started in the past and is still running.
