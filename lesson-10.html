Model Verbs (Lesson-10)

Should is an auxiliary verb – a modal auxiliary verb. We use should mainly to:

  • Give advice or make recommendations
  • Talk about obligation
  • Talk about probability and expectation
  • Express the conditional mood

We often use “can” to talk about the ability to do something in the present or future: (Lesson-10)

These past tense modals (could have and should have) are useful for expressing your present feelings about a past decision (or other action).
Could have and should have are sometimes called “modals of lost opportunities.” They work like a grammatical time machine. The simple past just tells what happened. Past modals tell what could have, and should have happened.

Vocabulary

Defeat verb (/dɪˈfiːt/) हराना    To win against someone in

a fight, war, or competition

Spill verb  /spɪl/ छलकना / गिरा देना to (cause to) flow, move, fall, or spread over the edge or outside the limits of something: 1- I spilled coffee on my silk shirt. 2- Let’s see if I can pour the juice into the glass without spilling it. 3-He dropped a bag of sugar and it spilled all over the floor. 4-Don’t pour too much;it might spill over.

Sniff /snɪf/ सूंघना/ नाक से सुड़कनाto smell something by taking air in through your nose: 1-He sniffed his socks to see if they needed washing. 2-Dogs love sniffing each other. 3-She sniffed at her glass of wine before tasting it. (Lesson-10)

Mortar noun /ˈmɔːr.tɚ/ (MIXTURE) a mixture of sand, water, and cement or lime that is used to fix bricks or stones to each other when building walls.

Forbid verb (/fɚˈbɪd/) मना करना / रोकना to refuse to allow something, especially officially, or to prevent a particular plan of action by making it impossible: 1-I forbid you to marry him! 2-You can’t forbid me to go. 3-His parents forbid him the wine. 4-If you want to go, I can’t forbid you.

Vocabulary

Moldy adjective /ˈmoʊl·di/ फफूंदी लगा हुआ (of food, esp. old food, or objects that have been left too long in warm, wet places) covered or filled with a soft green, blue, gray, or black growth, usually undesirable. 1- You should not have eaten moldy bread.

Spendthrift noun /ˈspend.θrɪft/ Someone who spends a lot of money in a way that wastes it: 1-Pedro hated his son when he was a spendthrift, and loves him when he is not.
