Make Of & Make from: Both give a sense of material, but there is difference

Make of: The material remains the same, but Make from: the material converts into other things. Ex- This house is made of brick/bridge is made of steel (steel & brick remain the same). 2- Wine is made from grapes/Butter is made from milk(here grapes and milk change their form.

About: Never use after “describe”, “explain” and “details. It is incorrect (Describe it/ Explain about it/ give some details about it)

It’s hard for me to + (verb)-Lesson-26

When saying that something is ‘hard for me’ you are informing someone that what you are talking about is difficult or challenging for you.

It’s hard for me to argue your point.
Is it hard for me to concentrate on the task?
It’s hard for me to consider your other options.
It’s hard for me to depend on you.
Is it hard for him to decide where to go tonight?
