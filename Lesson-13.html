Vocabulary Lesson-13

Stationary adjective /ˈsteɪ.ʃə.ner.i/ एक ही स्थान में ठहरा हुआ not moving, or not changing: 1- a stationary car/train. 2-The car collided with a stationary vehicle. 3- The bus crashed into a stationary vehicle.

Boundless adjective  /ˈbaʊnd.ləs/ असीम/अनंत having no limit: 1-She has boundless energy and enthusiasm. 2- The masses have boundless creative power. 3- He approached every task with a boundless zest/interest.

Incense noun  /ˈɪn.sens/ धूप/सुगंध a substance that is burnt to produce a sweet smell, especially as part of a religious ceremony: 1- an incense burner/stick. 2- Let’s burn incense in the memory of our pure past. 3- The scent of incense lingered in the air. 4- Mr. Sharma lit incense and chanted Sanskrit mantras. (Lesson-13)

Apex noun /ˈeɪ.peks/ (The HIGHEST POINT) 1- He was at the apex of his career. 2- At 37, she’d reached the apex of her career.

Stifle verb ˈstaɪ.fəl/ (NO AIR) To (cause to) be unable to breathe because you have no air: 1-He is said to have stifled his victim with a pillow. To prevent something from happening, being expressed, or continuing: 1-She stifled a cough/yawn/scream/sneeze. 2-I don’t know how I managed to stifle my anger. 3-We should be encouraging new ideas, not stifling them

Steadfast adj /ˈsted.fæst/ दृढ़, स्थिर,निरंतर staying the same for a long time and not changing quickly or unexpectedly: 1- They stood steadfast on their faith. 2-He remained steadfast in his belief that he had done the right thing.

Infer verb /ɪnˈfɝː/ (अनुमान लगाना) To form an opinion or guess that something is true because of the information that you have: 1- What do you infer from her refusal? 2-He can logically infer that if the battery is dead, then the horn will not sound.
