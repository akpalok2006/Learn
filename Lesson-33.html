Future Perfect Tense

The future perfect tense is used to demonstrate an action which is promised to be done by a certain time in the future.

पहचान: चूका होगा, चुकी होगी, चुके होंगे (In Future)

  1. I shall have eaten. मैं खा चुकूँगा.
  2. You will have finished the work by next Sunday. अगले रविवार  तक तुम इस काम को कर चुके रहोगे.
  3. You will have come before the match starts. मैच शुरू होने से पहले  तुम आ चुके होगे.
  4. I  shall not have eaten. मैं नहीं खा चुकूँगा.
  5. You will not have taken a bath before you read. पढने के पहले  तुम स्नान नहीं कर चुके रहोगे.
  6. Sita will not have finished her work before you go. तुम्हारे जाने से  पहले सीता अपना काम समाप्त नहीं कर चुकी रहेगी.
  7. The crops will not have spoiled before the rain starts. वर्षा शुरू  होने से पहले फसलें बर्बाद  नहीं हो चुकेंगी.
  8. Shall I have eaten? क्या मैं खा चुकूँगा?
  9. Will you have finished the work by Sunday next? क्या अगले  रविवार तक तुम इस काम को कर चुके होगे?
  10. Will you have learned the English by next year. क्या आप अगले साल तक अंग्रेजी सीख चुके होंगे?
  11. Will he cooked the food before you come? क्या आपके आने से पहले वह  खाना बना चूका होगा?
  12. I shall have reached home before rain starts. मैं बारिश शुरू होने से पहले घर पहुँच गया हूँगा।
  13. Will he not have lost his patience before he enters the  examination hall? क्या परीक्षा भवन में प्रवेश करने से पहले वह अपना  धैर्य नहीं खो चुकेगा? (lesson-33)

Vocabulary lesson-33

Rack one’s brain idiom: अपना दिमाग खराब करना to think very hard: I’ve been racking my brains all day, but I can’t remember her name.

Puzzle sth out:-To discover or understand something by thinking hard about it: 1-I still can’t puzzle out how I managed to spend so much money last month.

Puzzle over sth (माथापच्ची करना) To think hard about someone or something for a long time and try to understand them: 1-I’ve puzzled over this question for a while, and I’m still not sure what the right answer is.

Fully-fledged UK full-fledged-us adjective /ˌfʊl.i ˈfledʒd/ completely developed or trained: 1-After years of study, Tim is now a fully-fledged architect. 2-It’s becoming increasingly possible to run a fully-fledged business from home. 3-Within months, the student had become a fully-fledged instructor. (lesson-33)

Impel verb /ɪmˈpel/ प्रेरित करना/उत्तेजित करना To make someone feel that they must do something: 1-I wonder what it is that impels him to exercise all the time. 2- The manager impels the team to greater. 3-There are various reasons that impel me to that conclusion. 4-It impelled her toward her most creative acts.

Comprehensive adjective /ˌkɑːm.prəˈhen.sɪv/ complete and including everything that is necessary: 1-We offer you comprehensive training in all aspects of the business. 2-Is this list comprehensive or are there some names missing? 3-We offer a comprehensive service

Vocabulary

Comprehend verb/ˌkɑːm.prəˈhend/ समझना To understand something completely: 1-I fail to comprehend their attitude. 2-I’ll never comprehend why she did what she did. 3-The child read the story but does not comprehend its meaning. 4- I did not fully comprehend what had happened.

Timid adjective /ˈtɪm.ɪd/ डरपोक Shy and nervous; without much confidence; easily frightened: 1-John is a timid child. 2- My dog is a little timid – especially around other dogs. 3-Timid dogs bark most. 4-Deer are naturally timid creatures.

Enrich verb /ɪnˈrɪtʃ/ (IMPROVE) To improve the quality of something by adding something else: 1-Fertilizer helps to enrich the soil. 2-By reading, we enrich the mind. 3-Education can greatly enrich your life.

Bicker verb /ˈbɪk.ɚ/ झगड़ना, कलह करना, नोकझोक करना To argue about things that are not important: 1-Will you two stop bickering! 2- They’re always bickering with each other about/over their personal problems. 3-They kept bickering over who should answer the phone. 4-The children are always bickering.

Seduce verb /səˈduːs/ बहकाना, लुभाना,आकर्षित करना To persuade or cause someone to do something that they would not usually consider doing by being very attractive and difficult to refuse: 1-I wouldn’t normally stay in a hotel like this, but I was seduced by the fabulous location. 2-They were seduced into buying the washing machine by the offer of a free flight. To persuade or trick someone into doing something by making it very attractive: 1-Clever advertising would seduce more people into smoking. 2- Are you trying to seduce me?

Retch verb /retʃ/ उबकना, क़ै आना To react in a way as if you are vomiting: 1-The foul smell of his body made me retch. 2-The smell made me retch.
