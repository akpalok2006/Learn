Fumble verb /ˈfʌm.bəl/ To do something awkwardly, especially when using your hands: 1-He fumbled in his pockets for some change. 2-She fumbled around/about in her purse, looking for her key. 3- They fumbled around/about (= moved awkwardly) in the dark, trying to find their way out of the cinema. To have difficulty saying or thinking of suitable words: 1- I was fumbling for the right word. 2- My mind went blank, and I began to fumble for words.
