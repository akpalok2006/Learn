Vocabulary lesson-43

Passive Voice Rules:

  1. In Passive voice, the Subject may or may not be present.
  2. We can never make passive voice without a direct Object.
  3. In Passive voice, we always use the VB3 form.
  4. In Passive voice, we never use Do, Does, and Did.
  5. The active sentence in which Be, Being, Been are already present, we can never make the passive of that sentence
  6. We can not make the passive voice of “Get grammar”.

While making sentences in a passive voice, we have to take the helping verb from the following chart. Lesson-43

Past perfect tense. Lesson-43

For the rule, see the table mentioned above. (been + past participle (v3))

In passive voice, a subject may or may not be present.

  1. The vegetable had been sold costly.
  2. The house had been whitewashed.
  3. You had been invited to the party
  4. In the cold weather, the blanket had been distributed to the poor.
  5. Last year, the weather conditions had been forecasted accurately.
