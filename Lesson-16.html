Past Indefinite Tense/ Simple past Tense (lesson-16)

Past Indefinite Tense represents an action that occurred in the past or a habit of the past and uses the past form of the verb.

पहचान :- आ, ई, ए \ आथा, इथी, एथी|

Rules:

Sentences

  1. Why didn’t you apologize to your brother, he could have forgiven you? आपने अपने भाई से माफी क्यों नहीं मांगी, वह आपको माफ कर सकता था?
  2. Did you use to show tantrums in childhood, but no longer now? क्या तुम  बचपन में नखरे दिखाया करते थे, लेकिन अब नहीं?
  3. Did your teacher embarrass you in front of all? क्या आपके शिक्षक ने आपको सभी के सामने शर्मिंदा किया?
  4. You did not put in all your efforts in the exam, that’s why you failed. आपने अपने सभी प्रयासों को परीक्षा में नहीं डाला, इसीलिए आप असफल रहे।
  5. I did not invite my friend to the party so that he crashed the party. मैंने अपने मित्र को पार्टी में आमंत्रित नहीं किया, इसलिए वह बिन बुलाय पार्टी में चला गया
  6. Did you celebrate the new year with pomp and show and watched the movie? क्या आपने नए साल का जश्न धूमधाम से मनाया और फिल्म देखी? (Lesson-16)

Subject + began + infinitive.

or

Subject + started + v4 + object

To express a situation that starts happening, this structure is used.

  1. She began to laugh. / She started laughing. वह हंसने लगी।
  2. I began to talk with my friend. / I started talking with my friend. मैं अपने दोस्त से बात करने लगा।
  3. It began to rain. / It started raining. बरसात होने लगी|
  4. He started learning English. / He began to learn English. उन्होंने अंग्रेजी सीखना शुरू किया।
  5. I started watching the movie. / I began to watch the movie. मैं फिल्म देखने लगा।

Vocabulary

Shroud verb /ʃraʊd/ ढकना, ढांकना, छिपाना To hide something by covering or surrounding it: 1- Visitors have complained about the scaffolding that shrouds half the castle. 2-Suddenly, all the lights went out, and the house was shrouded in darkness. 3-The mist shrouding the valley had lifted by eight o’clock. A layer of something that covers or surrounds something: 1- Everything was covered in a thick shroud of dust.

Dissipate verb /ˈdɪs.ə.peɪt/ To (cause to) gradually disappear or waste: 1-The office boy tried to dissipate the smoke by opening a window of the meeting room. 2-They dissipate all night and sleep all day. 3-His anger dissipated as the situation became clear. 4- Our dream has dissipated

Descend verb /dɪˈsend/ नीचे उतरना, उतरना To go or come down: 1-He descended to the helipad (वह हेलीपैड पर उतरे). 2- She descended the stairs (वह सीढ़ियों से नीचे उतरी।). 3-It is easier to descend than to ascend (चढ़ने से नीचे उतरना आसान है। ). 4-Our plane started to descend(हमारा विमान नीचे उतरने लगा।).

Dabble verb /ˈdæb.əl/ (TRY)ऊपरी तौर से दिलचस्पी लेना, (पानी में खेलना, छपछपाना) To take a slight and not very serious interest in a subject, or try a particular activity for a short period: 1-Most people dabble their way through life, never deciding to mastery anything in particular. 2-They dabble in the stock market. 3-Magicians do not dabble, they work hard. 4-Children like to dabble in the water.
