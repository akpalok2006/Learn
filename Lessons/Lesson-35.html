Past Perfect Continuous Tense (Lesson-35)

पहचान: रहा था, रही थी,रहे थे,+ समय से

 Sentences lesson-35

I had been living there for four years.  मैं वहाँ चार वर्षो से रह रहा  था.    

We had been walking since morning. हमलोग सुबह से टहल रहे थे.

You had been learning French for two years. तुम दो वर्षो से फ्रेंच  सीख रहे थे.    

He had been reading in this college for three years. वह तीन वर्षो  से इस कॉलेज में पढ़ रहा था. (lesson-35)

The children had been making a noise for two hours. बच्चें दो  घंटें से हल्ला कर रहे थे

The boys had been playing since morning. लड़कें सुबह से खेल रहे थे. 

Rajeev had been suffering from fever for two years. राजीव दो वर्षो से बुखार से पीड़ित था.

Had I been living there for four years ? क्या मैं वहाँ चार वर्षो से रह रहा था ?     

Had we been walking since morning ? क्या हमलोग सुबह से टहल रहे थे ?        

Had you been learning French for two years ? क्या तुम दो वर्षो से फ्रेंच सीख रहे थे ? 

Had he not been reading in this college for three years ? क्या वह तीन वर्षो से इस कॉलेज में नहीं पढ़ रहा था ?  

Had the children not been making a noise for two hours? क्या बच्चें दो घंटें से हल्ला नहीं कर रहे थे?  

Had the boys not been playing since morning ? क्या लड़कें सुबह से नहीं खेल रहे थे ?

Had Rajeev not been suffering from fever for two years ?  राजीव दो वर्षो से बुखार से पीड़ित नहीं था ?     

Why had the children not been making a  noise for two hours? बच्चें  क्यों दो घंटें से हल्ला नहीं कर रहे थे?

Vocabulary

Shed noun /ʃed/ A small building, usually made of wood, used for storing things: 1-A tool/storage shed. 2-A cow shed. 3-We keep our bikes in the shed. 4-The shed was roofed with dried straw.

Shed verb /ʃed/ (past tense and past participle shed) To lose a covering, such as leaves, hair, or skin, because it falls off naturally, or to drop something in a natural way or by accident: 1-The trees shed their leaves in autumn. 2-They ran down to the water, shedding clothes as they went. 3-She shed a few tears at her daughter’s wedding.

Shed light on something: To provide information about something or to make something easier to understand: 1-Tracking technologies have shed light on the birds’ social nature. 2-Recent research has shed light on the causes of the disease. 3-Can you shed light on this problem?

Whack verb /wæk/ जोर से चोट मारना, ज़ोर से मारना To hit someone or something noisily: 1- He starts out with a big hammer and whacks the boulder as hard as he can. 2-He whacked the tree trunk with his stick. 3- She whacked him in the mouth.

Vocabulary

Prevalence noun /ˈprev.əl.əns/ (vogue/trend) प्रचलन The fact that something is very common or often happens: 1-the prevalence of smoking among teenagers.

Succumb verb /səˈkʌm/ झुक जाना, अधीन होना, दम तोड़ देना, सिर झुकाना To lose the determination to oppose something; to accept defeat: 1-We tend to succumb to the emotions (हम भावनाओं के आगे झुक जाते हैं). 2- Don’t succumb to the temptation to have just one cigarette. 3- We should not succumb to temptation.

Sap verb /sæp/ सोखना, निर्बल बनाना, शक्तिहीन करना To make someone weaker or take away strength or an important quality from someone, especially over a long period of time: 1- Depression can sap the energy and self-esteem of an individual.

Puny adjective  /ˈpjuː.ni/ Small; weak; not effective: 1- A puny little man. 2 -What a puny little creature! 3-They laughed at my puny efforts. 4- Don’t plan for a puny goal.

Lousy adjective /ˈlaʊ.zi/ बहुत खराब, घटिया Very bad: 1-lousy food/service. 2- A lousy book. 3- I had a lousy weekend. 5- I feel lousy – I’m going home.

Vocabulary

Bolt verb /boʊlt/ To move very fast, especially as a result of being frightened (चौंक कर भाग जाना): 1-Frightened by the car horn, the horse bolted. 2- A frightened rabbit will bolt for its hole. To eat food very quickly: 1- Don’t bolt your food like that – you’ll get indigestion. 2- Don’t bolt your food down like that. To lock a door or window by sliding a bolt across. 1- Don’t forget to bolt on the door.
