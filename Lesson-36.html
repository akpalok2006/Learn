Future Perfect Continuous Tense

पहचान: रहा होगा, रही होगी,रहे होंगे,+ समय से (In future)

Rule: Sub+will have been+vb1+ing+obj+for/from+time period

We never make the passive voice of this tense

It is hardly used in spoken English

Instead of since from is used

 He will have been doing this work for ten days. वह दस दिनों से यह काम करता हुआ रहेगा.         

You will have been sleeping.  तुम सोते हुए रहोगे .

 The dogs will have been barking for the whole night. कुते रात भर भूँकते रहेंगे .      

They will have been working for years. वे लोग वर्षो से काम करते रहेंग .

I shall have been doing something from the morning.  मैं सुबह से कुछ करता रहूँगा. We shall have been sitting in the examination hall from 9 o’clock.

हमलोग 9 बजे से परीक्षा भवन में बैठे रहेंगे.

I shall have been teaching in the school from 2025.  मैं 2025 से स्कूल में पढ़ाता रहूँगा.      

They will not have been working for years. वे लोग वर्षो से काम करते नहीं रहेंगे.

Vocabulary

Persist verb /pɚˈsɪst/ If an unpleasant feeling or situation persists, it continues to exist: 1- If the pain persists, consult a doctor. 2- The cold weather is set to persist throughout the week. To try to do or continue doing something in a determined but often unreasonable way: 1- If he persists in asking awkward questions, then send him to the boss. 2- If symptoms persist, seek medical attention.

Ladle verb /ˈleɪ.dəl/ कलछुल, करछुल से देना To put soup or other liquid food into bowls to give to people, using a ladle. 1- Ladle the soup over rice. 2-She dipped out the soup with a ladle.

Relent verb /rɪˈlent/ नर्म होना, तरस खाना, दया करना, मान जाना (succumb) To act in a less severe way towards someone and allow something that you had refused to allow before: 1- Her parents eventually relented and let her go to the party(उसके माता-पिता अंततः मान गए और उन्हें पार्टी में जाने दिया।). 2-The police will not relent in their fight against crime (अपराध के खिलाफ अपनी लड़ाई में पुलिस पीछे नहीं हटेगी).

Enslave verb /ɪnˈsleɪv/ वशीभूत करना, दास बना लेना, अपने वश में करना To control someone’s actions, thoughts, emotions, or life completely: We are enslaved by technology. 2-Women in this region were enslaved by poverty. 3- Her beauty enslaved many young men.

Feeble adjective /ˈfiː.bəl/ Weak and without energy, strength, or power: 1-It was my feeble attempt. 2-He was a feeble, helpless old man. 3- The little lamp gave only a feeble light.

Intractable adjective /ɪnˈtræk.tə.bəl/ Very difficult or impossible to control, manage, or solve: 1- We are facing an intractable problem. 2- An intractable child. 3- The economy still faces intractable issues.
