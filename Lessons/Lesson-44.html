Model passive voice

As we used the rules to convert the tenses into the passive voice. Similarly, the model verb will follow the rules.

To convert the simple model into the passive voice, we add the verb “be” and past participle.

In passive voice, a subject may or may not be present.

  1. The rules should be followed strictly.
  2. The report should be submitted on time.
  3. The use of Pesticides should be banned from eatables.
  4. The flight fare should be reduced.
  5. The road should be repaired.
  6. A meeting invite should be sent each month.

The same rules apply to the remaining models (can, may, might, could, ought to,

The passive voice of should have and could have.

Subject + should/could have + past participle.

Henceforth, we learned that after the helping verb “has/have”, we used been and verb past participle form. Similarly, after the simple model helping verb, we use “be” and verb past participle form.
