Vocabulary Lesson-38

Endure verb ( /ɪnˈdʊr/) सहना to suffer something difficult, unpleasant, or painful:- 1 It seemed impossible that anyone could endure such pain. 2-They could endure much pain. 3-I can’t endure loud music.

Nag verb /næɡ/ दोष ढूँढना, तंग करना, सिर खाना (nounपीछे पड़ने वाला) to annoy a person by making continual criticisms or suggestions: 1- My mother always nags me to get my hair cut. 2-I have never seen a nag like this one (मैंने ऐसा पीछे पड़ने वाला / सिर खाने वाला कभी नहीं देखा।). 3-How often do I have to nag at you to tidy your room? (आपके कमरे को साफ-सुथरा रखने के लिए मुझे कितनी बार आपको पीछे पड़ना पड़ता है?). It was only nagging you somewhere in the back of your mind.

Mess around (SPEND TIME) To spend time doing various things that are not important, without any particular purpose or plan: 1-My brother likes messing around with computers. 2-He spends all day messing around with the dogs instead of working. 3-Stop messing around watching the TV, come and help me. (टीवी देखने के चक्कर में मत पड़ो, आओ और मेरी मदद करो।) 4-I need to stop messing around and make some decisions. 5-She must be messing around the kitchen.

Lesson-38

Itching to do something: wanting to do something very much: 1- The kids are itching to go out and play. 2- He was itching to hear the results. (परिणाम सुनने के लिए उसे खुजली हो रही थी।) 3-I’m itching to tell you the news! 4-He’s itching to get back to work. (lesson-38)

Burst into tears: To suddenly start to cry: 1- She put down the telephone and burst into tears. 2- She read the letter and promptly burst into tears. 3- Laura burst into tears and ran out of the room.

Spill the beans: To tell people secret information: 1- So who spilled the beans about her affair with David?

Dissuade verb /dɪˈsweɪd/ न करने के लिए समझाना To persuade someone not to do something: 1- I tried to dissuade her from getting married. 2- I tried to dissuade her from leaving. 2- Doctors had tried to dissuade patients from smoking.
