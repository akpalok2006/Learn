Vocabulary (lesson-15)

Begin verb /bɪˈɡɪn/ (v2 Began /bɪˈɡæn/ v3 Begun /bɪˈɡʌn/) शुरू करना To start to happen: 1-What time does the movie begin?

Superstition noun (/ˌsuː.pɚˈstɪʃ.ən/) अंधविश्वास belief that is not based on human reason or scientific knowledge, but is connected with old ideas about magic, etc.

Adverse adjective  /ædˈvɝːs/ विपरीत Having a negative or harmful effect on something: 1-The match has been canceled because of adverse weather conditions. 2-They received a lot of adverse publicity/criticism about the changes. 3-So far the drug is thought not to have any adverse effects. 4-Adverse effect/impact/change Recent bad publicity has had an adverse effect on sales. 5-Adverse conditions/consequences/circumstances Many experts believe it may be too late to prevent adverse consequences of the world’s climate system.

Adversity noun /ədˈvɝː.sə.t̬i/ मुसीबत/विपत्ति/दुर्भाग्य A difficult or unlucky situation or event: 1- She was always cheerful in adversity. (Lesson-15)

Trait noun /treɪt/ विशेषता, ख़सियात, गुण A characteristic, esp. of a personality: 1-Confidence is a fine trait. Over-confidence isn’t. 2-But your greatest character trait is your honesty.

Predicament noun /prəˈdɪk.ə.mənt/हालात,कठिन परिस्थिति,संकट An unpleasant situation that is difficult to get out of: 1-Other companies are in an even worse predicament than ourselves. 2-She went to the office to explain her predicament . 3-Now I really was in a dire predicament.

Simple present tense/present indefinite tense/daily routine tense.

पहचान : ताहै, तिहै, तेहैं, ताहूं|

Sentences (lesson-15)

  1. Why do today’s children take undue advantage of their parents? आज के बच्चे अपने माता-पिता का अनुचित लाभ क्यों उठाते हैं?
  2. Why does the government keep the poor in the dark? सरकार गरीबों को अंधेरे में क्यों रखती है?
  3. Why does your sister get angry over petty issues? तुम्हारी बहन छोटी-छोटी बात गुस्सा क्यों हो जाती है?
  4. When do you go to Delhi by bus, do you jostle in the bus? जब आप बस से दिल्ली जाते हैं, तो क्या आप बस में धक्का मुक्की करते है?
  5. Wonder why does my mother get carried away in emotions, over everything? पता नहीं क्यों मेरी मां हर बात पर भावनाओं में बह जाती है?
  6. Why don’t you make your sister understand, nowadays, she puts on airs? तुम अपनी बहन को क्यों नहीं समझाते, आजकल वह भाव खाती है?
  7. I listen to my parents, do you do so? मैं अपने माता-पिता की बात सुनता हूं, क्या आप ऐसा करते हैं?
  8. He doesn’t think about the adverse results. वह प्रतिकूल परिणामों के बारे में नहीं सोचता

Subject + begin/s + infinitive (Lession-15)

This expression is used to express the statement when something starts happening.

  1. He begins to read / He starts reading. वह पढ़ना शुरू करता है। वह पढ़ने लगता है|
  2. I begin to go / I start going. मैं जाना शुरू करता हूँ| मैं जाने लगता हूँ।
  3. She begins doubting me / She starts doubting me. वह मुझ पर शक करने लगता है।
  4. I begin to make a customer understand. / I start making a customer understand. मैं ग्राहक को समझाना शुरू करता हूं
  5. He begins yelling at me. / He starts yelling at me. वह मुझ पर चिल्लाना शुरू कर देता है।
  6. She starts weeping. / She begins to weep. वह रोने लगती है।
  7. I start driving. / I begin to drive. मैं गाड़ी चलाना शुरू करता हूं। मैं गाड़ी चलने लगता हूँ
  8. She begins to take a bath./ She starts taking a bath. वह नहाने लगती है।
  9. I begin to sleep. I start sleeping. मुझे नींद आने लगती है|
  10. He begins to talk to me. / He starts talking to me. वह मुझसे बात करने लगता है।
