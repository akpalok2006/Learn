Sub कोई काम करने ही वाला है

“I am about to clean my room.” If you said this is about the future, you are correct. We use “about to” when we’re talking about something we will do very soon in the future. We’re not talking about far in the future. Likewise, we usually use “will” for that. Furthermore, we’re talking about very, very soon, like in the next five minutes

Sub+ Is\am\are+about to+vb1+obj

  1. I am about to go. मैं जाने वाला हूं
  2. He is about to leave the office.
  3. I am about to invite you to the party. मैं आपको पार्टी में आमंत्रित करने वाला हूं
  4. I am about to sleep. मैं सोने वाला हूं
  5. She is about to dance. वह नाचने वाली है
  6. I am about to reply to your email.
  7. I am about to switch off the TV.
  8. He is about to leave the office.
  9. This medicine is about to mitigate the pain.
  10. We are about to prepare an intriguing report.
  11. His business is about to thrive. (Lesson-22)

Vocabulary

Mitigate verb /ˈmɪt̬.ə.ɡeɪt/ to make something less harmful, unpleasant, or bad: 1- The doctor gave me a prescription to mitigate the pain.

Thrive verb /θraɪv/ कामयाब होना/फलना-फूलना to grow, develop, or be successful: 1-His business thrived in the years before the war. 2-A man can never thrive who had a wasteful wife. 3-A business cannot thrive without investment. 4-Children thrive on fresh air and good food. 5-I believe that your business will thrive more this year.

Intriguing adjective /ɪnˈtriː.ɡɪŋ/ दिलचस्प / लुभावना very interesting because of being unusual or mysterious: 1-She has a really intriguing personality. 2-It all sounds very intriguing. 3-I found the story rather intriguing.

Thrill verb /θrɪl/ रोमांच a feeling of extreme excitement, usually caused by something pleasant: 1-It gave me the thrill to know I had passed the examination. 2-His music continues to thrill audiences. (Lesson-22)

Conviction noun /kənˈvɪk.ʃən/ दृढ विश्वास A strong opinion or belief / A feeling of being certain about something:1-It’s my personal conviction that all rapists should be locked away for life. 2-He said he was enjoying his new job, but his voice lacked conviction.

Impetus noun /ˈɪm.pə.t̬əs/ प्रोत्साहन, प्रेरणा, Something that encourages a particular activity or makes that activity more energetic or effective: 1- This article provides the main impetus for change. 2-The discovery gave fresh impetus to the research.

Stratosphere noun /ˈstræt̬.ə.sfɪr/ An extremely high level: 1-Property prices have gone into the stratosphere. 2-It will boost your confidence and self-esteem level to the stratosphere
