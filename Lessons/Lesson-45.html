Vocabulary

Until preposition, conjunction (/ʌnˈtɪl/) जब तक (time limit) up to (the time that): Shouldn’t we wait until Antony’s here? 2-Once he starts painting a room, he won’t stop until it’s done. 3-She waited until her coffee had cooled down before taking a sip. 4-We didn’t arrive at our hotel until after midnight.

Until preposition, conjunction (DISTANCE): 1- Don’t whistle until you are out of the wood. 2-Stay on the bus until 57th Street and then walk one block west.

As long as: During the whole time that. जबतक. I’ll love you as long as I live (मैं जब तक जीवित रहूंगा, आपको प्यार करूंगा।)

By the time जबतक

Used for saying what has already happened at the time that something else happens
By the time we arrived, the other guests were already there.

By the time I got there, he’d gone. (जबतक मैं वहां पहुंचा वह चला गया था)

However, adverb (/ˌhaʊˈev.ɚ/). (हालाँकि) चाहे. Despite whatever amount or degree: However hungry I am, I never seem to be able to finish off a whole pizza.

However, adverb (DESPITE THIS): 1- This is one possible solution to the problem. However, there are others. 2-You won’t move that stone, however strong you are. 3- I’ll come however busy I am. 4-He won’t win, however hard he tries. 5-However carefully I explained, she still didn’t understand.

As far as जहां तक……… का सवाल है With “as far as” concerned is used. As far as unemployment’s concerned, a change in government would be a good idea (जहां तक ​​बेरोजगारी का सवाल है, सरकार में बदलाव एक अच्छा विचार होगा।). 2- As far as I’m aware, he has not committed this crime. 3-As far as I can remember, this is the third time we’ve met. 4-His parents supported him as far as they could.

Vocabulary lesson-45

In case noun ( /keɪs/) यदि / किसी सूरत में/ किसी स्थिति में. Because of the possibility of something happening, being needed, etc.: Bring a map in case you get lost.

Provided that बशर्ते की / पर शर्त यह है की. (on the condition that) if, or only if: 1- You may go to the party provided that you’re home by 12.00. 2- You can drive a car provided that you have a valid license

Suppose verb (/səˈpoʊz/) मान लीजिए to think that something is likely to be true: Dan didn’t answer his phone, so I suppose (that) he’s busy.

Quick-witted adjective (/ˌkwɪkˈwɪt̬.ɪd/) तर्कशील/ समझदार able to react quickly and skillfully: 1-She was quick-witted and had an extraordinarily agile mind (वह तेज-तर्रार थी और असाधारण रूप से चुस्त दिमाग की थी।). (lesson-45)

Ambiguity noun /ˌæm.bɪˈɡjuː.ə.t̬i/ अस्‍पष्‍टता (an example of) the fact of something having more than one possible meaning and therefore possibly causing confusion: 1-We wish to remove any ambiguity concerning our demands. 2- There is some ambiguity in the Bible story, and it has been interpreted in different ways.

Strive verb /straɪv/ कठोर परिश्रम करना (कुछ करने या पाने के लिए) to try very hard to do something or to make something happen, especially for a long time or against difficulties: 1-I will strive to overcome these problems. 2- Two dogs strive for a bone, and a third runs away with it.

Revenge noun /rɪˈvendʒ/ बदला/प्रतिशोध harm done to someone as a punishment for harm that they have done to someone else:1-He is believed to have been shot by a rival gang in revenge for the shootings last week. 2-He had been seeking a chance for revenge
