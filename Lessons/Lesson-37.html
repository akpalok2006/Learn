Compulsion Grammar:

Action is to be done by a person (Subject). There may be two situations – (1) The Subject wants to do that action willingly, and (2) The Subject does not want to do that action willingly. In the second situation, the person will do that action in compulsion. When there is any sort of compulsion in doing a thing or action, we express it by compulsion sentences. To express compulsion, ‘to’ is added after auxiliary verbs, as described below:

The action or state of forcing or being forced to do something.

Present Compulsion:

पहचान: Sub को कोई काम करना है/ करना पड़ता है/ करना पड़ रहा है. (lesson-37)

Stagger verb /ˈstæɡ.ɚ/ (बीमार, नशे में या वज़नदार चीज़ लिए होने के कारण) लड़खड़ाना, डगमगाना (छोटे-छोटे क़दमों से गिरते-पड़ते चलना).
