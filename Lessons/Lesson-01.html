Vocabulary of lesson-01

1. Auction noun (/ˈɑːk.ʃən/) (नीलाम करना) A usually public sale of goods or property, where people make higher and higher bids (= offers of money) for each thing until someone buys is paying most: (Lesson-01)

2. Mandatory adjective (/ˈmæn.də.tɔːr.i/ ) (बेहद जरुरी /अनिवार्य) Something that is mandatory one must do.

3. Ingrain verb [t] (/ɪnˈɡreɪn/) (चित्त में बैठा देना,गहराई तक पहुंचा हुआ ) to establish something such as a belief so firmly that it is not likely to change: 1- The fear of failure is deeply ingrained in most people. 2-It is ingrained in your head that you cannot speak English.

4. Theist noun (/ˈθiː.ɪst/) (आस्तिक, जो ईश्वर में विस्वास करे) Someone who believes in the existence of a god or gods

5. Atheist noun (/ˈeɪ.θi.ɪst/) (नास्तिक, जो ईश्वर में विस्वास न करे) Someone who does not believe in any God or gods

6. Facility noun /fəˈsɪl.ə.t̬i/ The buildings, equipment, and services provided for a particular purpose: 1-Shopping facilities. 2-Medical facilities. 3-Sports facilities. 4-Is there a call-back facility on this phone?

7. Spectacular adjective (/spekˈtæk.jə.lɚ/) very exciting to look at 1-A spectacular view 2-There was a spectacular sunset last. 3-The scenery is spectacular.

8-Literally adverb (/ˈlɪt̬.ɚ.əl.i/) using the real or original meaning of a word or phrase: 1-The event literally stopped the traffic. 2-Until the next payday, I was literally without any money. 3-They were responsible for literally millions of deaths

9-Astonishing adjective (/əˈstɑː.nɪ.ʃɪŋ/) very surprising: 1-There were an astonishing number of applicants for the job. 2-It’s astonishing that these criminals are free to walk the streets. 3-I find it quite astonishing that none of you liked the play.

10- Defuse verb [t] ( /dɪˈfjuz/) (तनाव कम करना) to make a difficult or dangerous situation calmer: 1- She defused the tension by changing the subject. 2- He was able to defuse his panic. 3- His quiet voice helped to defuse the situation.

General Sentences of lesson-01

11- What’s up : (क्या चल रहा है?) : Phrase. If you say to someone ‘What’s up?’ or if you tell them what’s up, you are asking them or telling them what is wrong or what is worrying them. (lesson-01)

12- Hang in there. : (Don’t give up): phrase. If you tell someone to hang in there or to hang on in there, you are encouraging them to keep trying to do something and not to give up even though it might be difficult.

13- To cut a long story short? : We use it when we are explaining what happened in a few words, without giving all the details: 1-He wanted to be a doctor but, to cut a long story short, he ended up as a pharmacist. 2-Long story short: she sold the painting.

14- Circle back: (To come back to or consider again) Definition: When your supervisor tells you he or she will “circle back” on an issue, it means ” to discuss it later. 1- They may not have the time to circle back on a prior incident.

15- By leaps and bounds: If something or someone grows or progresses in leaps and bounds or by leaps and bounds, they grow or progress very quickly. 1-My knowledge of German increased by leaps and bounds when I lived in Germany for a year. 2-Your kids have really grown by leaps and bounds in the past year.

16- Rabbit hole (idiom) A complicated or difficult situation, especially one that is difficult to get out of. 1- She once went down that political rabbit hole. 2-Their advice is not meant to send you down a rabbit hole. 3-Let’s avoid that particular rabbit hole.

17- It’s below my dignity : (यह मेरे शान के खिलाफ है) : Definition of beneath one’s dignity. : suitable for someone who is considered less important He thought washing dishes was beneath his dignity.

General Sentences of lesson-01

18- It’s a hard nut to crack : (यह तो नको चने चबाने के बराबर है) : A difficult problem; also, an individual who is difficult to deal with. For example, This assignment is a hard nut to crack, or It won’t be easy getting her approval; she’s a tough nut to crack.

19- It’s a piece of cake : (यह तो बहुत ही आसान काम है) : The saying “a piece of cake” means something that’s simple to accomplish.

20- It’s not my cup of tea : (यह मेरे वस् की बात नहीं है): ‘It is an idiom – if something is not your cup of tea, you do not like it, or you are not interested in it.

21- By hook or by crook : (किसी भी क़िमत पर) by any method possible: 1-I want to change my job by hook or by crook.

22- Pissed off (adjective) (/ˌpɪst ˈɑːf/): Pissed off definition: annoyed, irritated, or disappointed. 1- The teacher was pissed off at the late student. 2-He is pissed off at me. 2-we’re very very pissed off.

General Sentences

23- Please put on a sweater otherwise you will catch a cold. ( कृपया स्वेटर पहन लो नहीं तो आपको ठण्ड लग जाएगी) : The phrase to catch a cold means to become infected with a common cold virus. It is sometimes shortened to catch a cold.

24- My exams are around the corner.: (मेरी परीक्षाएं नजदीक है): Around the corner/round the corner. Phrase. If you say that something is around the corner, you mean that it is very near.

24- Nowadays lady luck is smiling at him(आज कल उसकी किस्मत उसपर मेहरबान है) “Lady Luck is smiling at you today”, just means all the random uncontrollable things in your life are going to go well
