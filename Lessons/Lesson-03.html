Nail it : Idiom (lesson-03)

When you perform a task well, or you have done it perfectly without any mistakes, it means you have nailed it. So if you get something right, you nail it as you do it best or impressively.

When a person is about to do something, and you tell him, “nail it”, it means you are telling him to do it well. This idiom is also used when a problem is solved successfully or a perfect solution to a problem or an issue is discovered.

1–The computer wizard sat up all night to nail the hardware issue. 2- The police nailed the criminal after long hours of investigation and interrogation. 3-Do you nail every task you undertake?
